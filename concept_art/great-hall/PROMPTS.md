# Great Hall concept-art prompts

All four images were generated with the built-in image-generation tool. Each
prompt used these two local reference images:

1. `assets-src/gr1/home-island-source.png` — authoritative home-island palette,
   architecture, materials, and pixel-art style; its magenta background was
   explicitly excluded.
2. `concept_art/wayfinders exploration ui concept sheet.png` — pixel-art UI,
   timber, rope, parchment, nautical pictograms, and interface hierarchy.

## A — Ancestor Wall

```text
Use case: ui-mockup
Asset type: 16:9 game UI concept art for the Wayfinders Great Hall, option A — "Ancestor Wall"
Input images: Image 1 is the authoritative home-island pixel-art style, palette, architecture, and material reference; ignore its magenta background. Image 2 is the UI reference for crisp pixel art, timber/rope/parchment construction, nautical pictograms, and readable game-interface hierarchy.
Primary request: Show a practical graphical lineage chronicle inside the central timber hall of the tropical home island. The screen has two scales. Across the back wall is one paged ERA of twelve navigator memorial portraits in a compact 3-by-4 gallery, with previous/next carved canoe-prow controls and a small bead-track showing that many more eras exist. One selected navigator expands into a large painted pixel-art bust. Beneath the bust are exactly four compact voyage bands, each dominated by clear achievement glyphs: dotted canoe route, enclosed lagoon ring, island silhouette, surveyed island ring, outline site marker, filled site marker, fish ripple, broken mast, and rare gold shell-idol. A shell-and-rope lineage totals strand runs along the top using pictograms plus tiny numbers. Visually distinguish active, completed, lost-at-sea, and later-confirmed lost navigators through material treatment: fresh turquoise paint, warm patina, dark cracked wood, and repaired shell inlay.
Scene/backdrop: warmly lit interior of the same rust-roof timber Great Hall seen from the front, woven wall panels, basalt footings, heavy posts, subtle firelight and turquoise daylight from the harbor.
Style/medium: polished shippable game UI mockup in crisp detailed pixel art, matching the reference scale and palette; not loose concept painting.
Composition/framing: landscape desktop screen, clear large selected portrait on one side and compact era gallery plus four voyage bands on the other; usable at a glance; physical wall artifacts form the interface.
Lighting/mood: warm, reverent, accumulated history; deep teal shadows, timber brown, roof rust, sand cream, foliage green, turquoise and shell-gold accents.
Text: nearly none; only tiny unobtrusive generation numerals and a simple era fraction may appear. Let pictures, state treatments, glyphs, tally marks, and spatial grouping communicate.
Constraints: show navigator pictures clearly; show a large lineage rather than only one generation; exactly four voyage positions for the selected navigator; every icon must have a strong distinct silhouette; minimal text; invented fictional island-culture ornament based on sea, canoe, shell, weaving, wood, and navigation; no direct copy of any identifiable real-world Indigenous or sacred motif; no photorealism; no generic glass dashboard; no logos; no watermark.
Avoid: paragraphs, tables of text, modern cards, neon sci-fi styling, tiny illegible clutter, parchment covering the whole screen.
```
