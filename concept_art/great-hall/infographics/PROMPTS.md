# Great Hall infographic image prompts

All sheets used `concept-a-refined-ancestor-wall.png` as the primary material,
palette, and composition reference and
`concept_art/wayfinders exploration ui concept sheet.png` as the icon/UI
reference. These prompts are retained so later production work can reproduce
or deliberately revise the art direction.

## 01 — Lineage counting cord

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: Wayfinders Great Hall infographic art sheet 01 — Lineage Counting Cord
Primary request: Draw a polished game-ready concept sheet of exactly fourteen distinct lineage tally pictograms, arranged as two clean rows of seven equal carved timber-and-sailcloth tokens connected by one shell-and-rope counting cord. In exact left-to-right order: 01 gold shell-idol inside an open progress ring; 02 three overlapping navigator portrait silhouettes; 03 canoe bow entering a two-post home harbor; 04 portrait plaque above four closed voyage notches; 05 broken canoe beneath one wave; 06 dotted canoe wake connecting two safe-water marks; 07 closed lagoon ring; 08 outline island with two sight rays; 09 inlaid island inside a closed survey ring; 10 open coastal survey marker; 11 filled coastal survey marker; 12 outline fish above an open ripple; 13 filled fish above a closed ripple; 14 broken mast joined by a bright shell knot.
Style/medium: crisp detailed pixel art matching the references, strong readable silhouettes, limited colors per icon, physical shell/wood/sailcloth construction.
Composition/framing: 16:9 orthographic presentation sheet, exactly 14 equally sized tokens, generous separation, consistent icon scale, dark teal woven-mat background, warm timber border.
Color palette: deep teal, timber brown, rust, sand cream, turquoise and seafoam; shell gold only for token 01 and the repair knot in token 14.
Text: render only the exact two-digit identifiers "01" through "14", once beneath their matching tokens; no names or other text.
Constraints: every icon must remain distinguishable in grayscale by shape and fill; no duplicate silhouettes; invented Wayfinders sea-navigation grammar; no feathers, faces, culturally identifiable sacred motifs, generic fantasy runes, modern glass cards, logos or watermark.
```

## 02 — Era navigation rail

```text

A bright turquoise shell marks the selected generation within the twelve. Include a large left-facing carved canoe-prow button, a large right-facing carved canoe-prow button, a distinct small home-harbor shell shortcut for the current navigator, and a compact recessed direct-generation control with a simple number stone. Show the current era range with only the exact small numerals "37–48" and the position with only "4 / 9".
Style/medium: polished shippable game UI mockup in crisp detailed pixel art, timber, rope, shells, woven sailcloth and turquoise inlay, matching the references.
Composition/framing: 16:9 close-up orthographic sheet, one centered rail filling the width with all controls visible and generous clickable spacing, dark teal woven background and warm timber frame.
Constraints: exactly twelve prominent current-era knots; chronological direction must be obvious; previous, next, current and direct-jump controls must have different silhouettes; minimal text limited to "37–48" and "4 / 9"; no portraits, no achievement icons, no feathers, no culturally identifiable sacred motifs, no fantasy runes, no modern glass UI, no logos or watermark.
```

Final correction prompts:

```text
Change only the upper current-era knot row. Replace the current numbered shell row with exactly twelve evenly spaced prominent shell knots. The fourth shell from the left must be bright turquoise to mark selection; the other eleven must be cream shell. Remove all individual knot-number labels. Preserve every other element.

Add exactly one cream shell knot to the upper rope, in the open space immediately to the right of the current last cream shell. After this single addition there must be exactly twelve shell knots total: three cream shells, one turquoise shell, then eight cream shells. Preserve every other element.
```
