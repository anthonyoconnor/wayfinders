# Wayfinders living-sea concept studies

These three studies explore non-combat sea-animal encounters at the
world-gameplay scale. They are concept art, not runtime assets, approved
creature designs, or authorized gameplay behavior. The possible design space
is recorded in `docs/Wayfinders_Possible_Future_Features.md`; the current
roadmap remains the sole owner of planning and authorization status.

The shared visual thesis is: keep the sea spacious, the vessel small, and the
animal behavior readable while using observation distance, wakes, weather,
and migration paths to communicate play without weapons, health bars, capture,
or boss framing.

All three images were generated on 2026-07-20 with the built-in
image-generation tool. No existing image was used as an edit target or style
reference.

## Studies

1. `old-shellback-observation.png` - an immense turtle reads as moving
   geography while a lone canoe charts its migration from a respectful
   distance.
2. `stormwhale-weather-reading.png` - a surfacing whale and the edge of a
   regional storm create a legible choice between following the sign and
   turning toward clear water.
3. `lantern-ray-migration.png` - a canoe follows outside a ray congregation's
   turning arc, presenting calm behavioral observation as a sailing activity.

## Final prompts

### Old Shellback observation

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: Wayfinders game concept art, landscape exploration scene
Primary request: create a polished concept painting of a non-combat encounter with "The Old Shellback," an immense ancient sea turtle whose weathered shell resembles a small overgrown island. A tiny single-sail Wayfinders canoe keeps a respectful distance and traces the creature's migration rather than attacking it.
Scene/backdrop: spacious tropical archipelago ocean viewed from a high three-quarter/top-down game-like angle; deep navy water opening into turquoise shallows; one distant organic island silhouette; broken foam and short wavelets; large quiet regions of sea.
Subject: the enormous turtle is calm and dignified, partially submerged, with a legible turtle silhouette beneath water, a shell carrying sparse wind-shaped plants, pale shell inlay-like natural markings, and seabirds resting on it. The canoe is clearly much smaller and leaves a restrained curved wake outside the animal's personal space.
Style/medium: painterly game concept art with crisp readable silhouettes and subtle pixel-art sensibility in clustered marks; handmade, observant, warm maritime fantasy; production concept study, not a final runtime sprite.
Composition/framing: wide cinematic landscape; turtle as primary focal point, canoe secondary, distant island tertiary; generous negative space; navigation relationship readable immediately.
Lighting/mood: warm late-afternoon sun, calm wonder, inviting uncertainty rather than menace.
Color palette: navy and deep teal distance, turquoise and seafoam coast/wake, shell ivory and weathered ochre highlights, warm timber and rust sail accents.
Materials/textures: salt-worn shell, organic algae and scrub, woven sailcloth, lashed timber, interrupted foam.
Constraints: no combat, no weapons, no injury, no health bars, no boss framing, no attacking pose, no glowing magical runes, no UI, no text, no logos, no watermark, no culturally specific sacred motifs. The creature must feel like moving geography and a being to observe, not an enemy.
```

Color palette: deep navy, charcoal, indigo, rain-muted teal, turquoise safe water, shell-ivory foam, restrained amber on the canoe and horizon.
Materials/textures: layered rain curtains, broken foam, low wavelets, wet sailcloth, salt-worn timber, natural whale skin with subtle scars.
Constraints: no combat, no weapons, no attack, no open jaws, no teeth display, no injury, no monster aggression, no lightning strike, no health bars, no boss framing, no glowing magic, no UI, no text, no logos, no watermark, no culturally specific sacred motifs. The scene must communicate reading weather and choosing a route.
```

### Lantern Ray migration

Color palette: indigo and deep teal water, muted turquoise shallows, seafoam wake, shell-ivory highlights, sparse pale cyan on rays, warm timber and ochre sail.

The image must communicate observation, distance, and route-reading.
```

## Review boundary

The studies intentionally explore mood, silhouette, spatial relationship, and
non-combat interaction. They do not establish species catalogs, scale,
movement, migration, clue, storm, collision, audio, reward, knowledge,
Great Hall, or encounter contracts. Generated details must not be treated as
gameplay truth, and any later runtime asset would require the normal production
asset workflow and target-scale review.
