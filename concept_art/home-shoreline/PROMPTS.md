# Home-island shoreline concept prompts

All three concepts were created with the built-in image-generation tool.
`reference-current-game.png` was used as the edit target and exact composition,
scale, and style reference.

## Shared prompt

```text
Use case: precise-object-edit
Asset type: top-down pixel-art game environment concept
Input images: Image 1 is the edit target and exact composition/style reference.
Primary request: change only the surrounding water and shallow-water transition around Home Island.
Style/medium: preserve the supplied detailed clustered pixel-art look, orthographic top-down view, camera framing, island scale, island pixels, dock, label, fog, and UI exactly.
Water rendering: keep one continuous fine wave-detail layer across all depths; use crisp clustered pixels and broken dithered palette thresholds, not a photographic blur.
Color ramp: at least five perceptible stages from pale mint/seafoam through luminous aqua and turquoise to blue-teal and the existing deep navy; small value steps with no single hue or luminance cliff.
Constraints: no square tiles, no rectangular opacity blocks, no chunk seams, no hard contour line, no uniform coastline-following halo, no continuous foam ring, no new land, boats, structures, labels, text, logos, or watermark. Preserve everything except water.
```

## A — Bahamian sand shelf

```text
Concept direction: asymmetric Bahamian sand shelf with patch reefs.
Make the bright sandy shelf broad and lobed south/southwest and through the protected east harbor, but narrow off rocky north and west headlands. Cut one natural sinuous slightly darker teal navigation channel eastward from the dock, widening into open water. Add two detached pale crescent sand shoals and three to five restrained dark-teal coral or seagrass patches, mainly southeast and southwest. The outer shelf edge must be low-frequency, soft, offset from shore, and visibly non-uniform, with roughly three-to-one width variation. Cozy tropical read; the island remains the focus.
```

## B — Broken fringing-reef lagoon

```text
Concept direction: broken fringing-reef lagoon.
Keep a narrow irregular bright shore wash, then a calm medium-aqua lagoon whose width varies strongly. Place a broken offshore reef crescent mostly north and east, with multiple generous gaps and two darker deep-water fingers entering the lagoon. Scatter a few muted coral heads and sand channels. Use sparse broken breaker flecks only along exposed reef crests, never as a complete ring. The reef silhouette must be organic and offset from the coast; the island remains the focus.
```

## C — Cove-led coastal patchwork

```text
Concept direction: cove-led coastal patchwork without an enclosing shelf.
Concentrate luminous shallows in the east harbor cove and along the south beach, while blue-teal/deeper water approaches the rocky north and west points. Use irregular kidney-shaped pale sand pools, a hooked submerged sand spit extending from a southern point, sparse darker reef/seagrass patches, and sinuous deep-water fingers between them. There must be no complete shallow ring at any distance from the island. Keep the composition readable and warmly tropical at map scale; the island remains the focus.
```
