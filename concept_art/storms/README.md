# Wayfinders storm-system concept studies

These five studies explore how natural storms could read in Wayfinders at the
world-gameplay scale. They are concept art, not runtime assets or approved
gameplay behavior. Feature contracts still own weather rules, hazards,
visibility, movement, damage, and forecasting.

The shared visual thesis is: keep the crisp high-angle pixel-gameplay language,
organic coastal silhouettes, deep teal water, luminous shallows, and selective
warm craft accents while letting cloud shadow, precipitation, wind, sea state,
spray, runoff, and shelter make each storm spatially legible.

All five images were generated on 2026-07-19 with the built-in image-generation
tool. `../style-guide/example-01-refined-pixel-gameplay.png` was supplied as a
style and gameplay-camera reference only, never as an edit target.

## Studies

1. `storm-concept-01-home-shore-squall.png` — a fast coastal squall divides
   Home Shore between rain-darkened exposure and a warm readable harbor.
2. `storm-concept-02-open-ocean-lightning-front.png` — a small sailing canoe
   approaches a curved boundary between quiet teal water and a rough lightning
   front.
3. `storm-concept-03-mangrove-lagoon-deluge.png` — a compact tropical cell
   floods one side of a branching lagoon while the sheltered lee stays clear.
4. `storm-concept-04-basalt-cove-thunderstorm.png` — heavy rain, runoff,
   lightning, and cliff spray make a basalt cove read as natural shelter.
5. `storm-concept-05-winter-atoll-squall.png` — a diagonal sleet front crosses
   an ice-blue atoll and contrasts with the warm waystation inside its lagoon.

## Review boundary

The studies deliberately emphasize readable environmental cause and effect:
cloud cover changes value and visibility, wind changes foliage and wakes, rain
changes water texture and runoff, and shore geometry changes exposure. They do
not prescribe mechanics, damage values, encounter rules, or forecast UI.
